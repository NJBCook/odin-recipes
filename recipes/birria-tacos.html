Birria Tacos

Description

Birria tacos are a culinary delight from Mexico, featuring tender, slow-cooked meat marinated in a vibrant blend of dried chiles, spices, and herbs. The meat, cooked until melt-in-your-mouth tender, is shredded and served in warm corn tortillas, topped with fresh onions, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime, creating a perfect balance of smoky, spicy, and savory flavors.

Accompanied by a rich consommé made from the meat’s cooking juices, these tacos are dipped before each bite, enhancing their depth and complexity. Optional garnishes like avocado slices and thinly sliced radishes add a beautiful and delicious touch, making birria tacos a truly unforgettable culinary experience.

Ingredients

For the Birria Sauce:

  • 5 dried guajillo chiles, stemmed and seeded
  • 3 dried ancho chiles, stemmed and seeded
  • 3 dried pasilla chiles, stemmed and seeded
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
  • 4 garlic cloves
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground cloves
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Meat:

  • 1.5 kg (3 lbs) beef chuck roast, cut into large chunks
  • 1 kg (2 lbs) short ribs or oxtail
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

For the Tacos:

  • Corn tortillas
  • Chopped white onion
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Lime wedges
  • Queso fresco or mozzarella cheese, shredded (optional)

For Serving:

  • Birria consommé (the cooking liquid)
  • Salsa (optional)

Garnish (Optional):

  • Avocado slices
  • Radishes, thinly sliced

Steps

  1. Start by making the birria sauce. Toast the dried chiles in a skillet over medium heat until fragrant, about 1-2 minutes on each side.
  2. Place the toasted chiles in a bowl and cover them with hot water. Let them soak for 15-20 minutes until they are soft.
  3. In a blender, combine the soaked chiles, beef broth, onion, garlic, tomatoes, apple cider vinegar, ground cumin, dried oregano, ground cinnamon, ground cloves, salt, and pepper. Blend until smooth.
  4. Strain the sauce through a fine-mesh sieve into a large bowl to remove any solids. Set aside.
  5. Season the beef chuck roast and short ribs (or oxtail) with salt and pepper.
  6. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Sear the meat on all sides until browned.
  7. Add the birria sauce to the pot, making sure the meat is fully submerged. If necessary, add more beef broth or water to cover the meat.
  8. Add the bay leaves to the pot. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 2.5 to 3 hours, or until the meat is tender and shreds easily.
  9. Remove the meat from the pot and shred it using two forks. Return the shredded meat to the pot and stir to combine with the sauce.
  10. To make the tacos, heat a skillet or griddle over medium heat. Dip each corn tortilla in the birria consommé, then place it on the skillet.
  11. Top each tortilla with shredded meat, chopped white onion, fresh cilantro, and cheese if desired. Fold the tortilla in half and cook until crispy, about 2-3 minutes per side.
  12. Serve the tacos hot, with a side of birria consommé for dipping, lime wedges, salsa, and optional garnishes like avocado slices and radishes.
